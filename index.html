True or False: SIDS can be prevented.

  1. True
  2. False

Wrong!!!

Until we know what causes SIDS, it can't be 100% prevented. We can however reduce the risk of death from outside stressers.

CORRECT

False! While SIDS can't be prevented there are things that can be done to help reduce the risk of SIDS happening. Question 2:

Where is the safest place for baby to sleep?

  1. In their own crib
  2. In bed with a parent
  3. Where ever baby falls asleep

Q2 CORRECT!!

According to the AAP, infants should sleep in a safety-approved crib, portable crib, play yard, or bassinet. Their sleep area should be without blankets, crib bumpers, stuffed animals, or sleep positioners. http://pediatrics.aappublications.org/content/128/5/e1341.full.html#sec-16

Q2 WRONG!!

Infants should never sleep with a parent. Bed sharing can increase the risk of SIDS as well as accidental suffocation of the infant. http://pediatrics.aappublications.org/content/128/5/e1341.full.html#sec-19

Q2 WRONG!!

Babies should be placed in a safe sleep environement once asleep. Carseats, rockers, couches, and other sleep surfaces are dangerous and can increase a babies risk of accidental suffocation and SIDS.
